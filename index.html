      Case Study

      Branding | Design

      Role: Product Designer

      Responsibilities: Strategy, User Research, Usability Testing, UX Design, UI Design, Wireframing, Prototyping, Persona Development, Branding

      Tools: Pen & Paper | Sketch | Flinto

      Picolo came about as a merging of my two passions: good food and discounts on good food.

      Problem 1

      Most restaurant reward/loyalty programs do not target, engage or retain their best customers. The financial effectiveness of a rewards/discount programs is premised on its ability to increase the patronage and retention of its customers. However, research has shown that many customers in fact do not return after visiting a restaurant on a discounted visit.

      Moreover, long term engagement with loyalty programs is a significant issue, with engagemnt dropping drastically after a couple of month. Even a nominal increase in customer retention can drastically affect a restaurant’s bottom line (cite study) and restaurants who do no target their most active patrons are missing out on a significant source of revenue.

      Problem 2.

      Reward programs require “disruptive” behavior i.e. “checking in”, remembering to use a loyalty card, taking a survey

      Solution

      Create a rewards program that targets a restaurant’s most active and frequent patrons without disrupting the normal flow of a meal.

      Picolo enables restaurants to identify and reward their best customers by utilizing a normal customer behavior to gain real time insight into the customer experience. What is that behavior?

      Taking a picture of your appetizer/entree/drink/dessert/all of the above.

      People who take pictures of their food are usually the most active, frequent patrons of a restaurant. For these patrons, the experience is just as important as the meal. A positive experience at a restaurant is the best indicator of a potential for a repeat visit. The best bet for a restaurant is to target it customers who visit the most and have positive interactions with a restaurant.

      By providing a reward for normal patron behavior, Picolo provides a unique opportunity for a restaurant to engage its most active patrons.
