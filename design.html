    Tools: Pen & Paper | Sketch | Marvel | Lookback

    Brief

    Most restaurant reward/loyalty programs do not target, engage or retain their best customers. The financial effectiveness of a rewards/discount programs is premised on its ability to increase the patronage and retention of its customers. However, research has shown that many customers actually do not return after visiting a restaurant on a discounted visit. (1, 2)

    Moreover, long term engagement with loyalty programs is a significant issue, with engagement dropping drastically after a couple of months. Even a nominal increase in customer retention can drastically affect a restaurant’s bottom line (cite study) and restaurants who do no target their most active patrons are missing out on a significant source of revenue.

    Reward programs require “disruptive” behavior i.e. “checking in”, remembering to use a loyalty card, taking a survey.

    Solution

    Create a rewards program that targets a restaurant’s most active and frequent patrons without disrupting the normal flow of a meal.

    Picolo enables restaurants to identify and reward their best customers by utilizing a normal patron behavior to gain real time insight into the customer experience. Namely, taking pictures of one's appetizer/entree/drink/dessert/all of the above.

    Though they are occasionally stigmatized by both restaurateurs and society at-large, people who take pictures of their food are usually the most active, frequent patrons of a restaurant. For these patrons, the experience is just as important as the meal. A positive experience at a restaurant is the best indicator of a potential for a repeat visit. The best bet for a restaurant is to target it customers who visit the most and have positive interactions with a restaurant.

    By providing a reward for normal patron behavior, Picolo provides a unique opportunity for a restaurant to engage its most active patrons.

    Persona Development

    In order to condense the research conducted on restaurant reward programs and focus on the major needs of users, I created a primary and secondary user persona. Throughout the course of the design process, I referenced the personas to determine feature prioritization and guide my design decisions.

  • Ubani

    The World in Your Pocket

    Role: Product Designer

    Responsibilities: Strategy, User Research, Competitive Analysis, Usability Testing, UI, Wireframing, Prototyping, Branding

    Tools: Pen & Paper | Sketch | Marvel | Lookback

    Brief

    With the burgeoning sophistication of mobile content delivery systems and the increasing demand for unique, curated travel experiences, now more than ever, people are using audio tours to experience of a tour guide while retaining control over the pace and direction of the tour. While the content of audio tour guides is usually high quality, the applications that deliver the content leave something to be desired. In Boston, there is an abundance of audio tour apps, but few incorporate basic mobile technology such as location-tracking and many have not been updated in years. For an audio guided tour, location awareness is essential to providing the proper context for what the user is seeing. Through concept testing and user research, I found that many other people share the same frustration with the lack of usable audio tour apps.

    Solution

    Location aware audio guides provide an opportunity for the user to define their own experience and enable the agency and authorship of the visitor. Create a simple, unubtrusive location aware application that provides consistently high quality experience for the user.

    Research

    From the research that I conducted, I was able to pinpoint user pain points and isolate gaps in the market. I realized that while Boston is a city full of history and culture, there were few apps that provided walking tours for boston. The apps that did exist provided tours for specific locations (e.g Freedom Trail) and had not be updated in 3+ years. Feedback gathered from iTunes reviews provided helpful insights into the strengths and weakness of competitors.
